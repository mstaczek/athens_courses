Course title:
Biotechnological Applications of Stem Cells: no smoke and mirrors.
Type of course:
Classic ATHENS course
Course code:
UPM137
Minimum year of study:
2
Language of tutition:
English
 
Prerequisites:

The required knowledge for correct understanding of the course includes basic concepts of cellular and molecular biology. Students should also have their own laptop available for projects outside of the classroom, computers will be provided in class whenever necessary.
Objectives:

The proposed course will study the origin, classification, sources and applications of stem cells, with particular focus on the current limitations of this material as well as their future applications. To this end we will center the course on the following concrete objectives:

a. Study and compare the characteristics and applications of stem cells base d on their distinct origin.

b. Present and evaluate the different specific methods for stem cell application: advantages and limitations.

c. Study and compare the characteristics of normal stem cells to tumoral stem cells.

d. Discuss ethical limitations and considerations of their study and use.

Programme to be followed:
Docs

Introduction to the course. Presentation of contents, methodology and evaluation method. Introduction to the the Moodle workspace.

1. Regenerative medicine. Concept of stem cell and quiescent cell. Potential applications of stem cells in regenerative therapy (1 hour).

Discussion of practical applications currently in use: advantages and disadvantages

1.1 Cardiac disease and the hematopoietic system (1 hour)

1.2 Skin (1 hour)

1.3 Diabetes (1 hour)

1.4 Central nervous system (1 hour)

2. Characteristics of stem cells.

2.1 Self-renewal signals and signal reception

2.1.1 Growth factors and mitogens: SCF, MG-SCF, G-CSF, EGF, bFGF, FGF8, Soni Hedgehog (2 hours)

2.1.2 Self-renewal signalling pathways: Wnt, Notch, BMI, PEDF (2 hours)

2.2 Cell cycle: cyclins, Cdks, kinase inhibitors. Role in proliferation and self-renewal (2 hours)

2.3 Survival vs apoptosis in quiescent cells. Nfkb, PI3K, p38, MAPK, AKT (2 hours)

3. Umbilical cord stem cells. 2 applications and technologies: VidaCord and LIFELENGTH (1 hour)

3.1 Visit VidaCord (2 hours)

3.2 Visit Promega (2 hours)

4. Concept of the cancer stem cell (1 hour)

4.1 Origin of the neoplastic event. Epigenetics vs mutation (1 hour)

4.2 Relapse and minimal residual disease. Directed drugs. Antibodies in anti-neoplastic therapy (1 hour)

4.3 Resistance to chemotherapy and radiotherapy (1 hour theory, 1 hour discussion)

4.4 Effects of chemotherapeutics on cancer stem cells versus tumoral cells (1 hour)

  • Lab 1: Purification of cáncer stem cells using immunomagnetism. Study of cell cycle and self-renewal via dose-response assays. Part I (2 hours)
  • Lab 2: Isolation of hematopoietic stem cells (2 hours)
  • Lab 3: Study of cell cycle and self-renewal via dose-response assays. Quantification using MTT. Part II (2 hours)
Course exam:
Evaluation of course contents will be done via a multiple-choice test and a project in the form of a Scientific poster. Oral presentation of the poster is optional. The poster can be submitted electronically up to 30 days after the course sesión has ended.
