Course title:
Aeroacoustics

English
 
Prerequisites:
Fluid Mechanics, Aerodynamics, Acoustics, Compressible Flow, Computational Aerodynamics, Computational Fluid Dynamics.
Objectives:
To understand the theory of aerodynamic noise generation; understand prediction and control of jet noise emissions, boundary layer noise, rotor noise and other common aerodynamic noise sources.
Programme to be followed:

1.     The Wave theory of Sound: Equations of fluid motion; Equations of Linear Acoustics; The Wave Equation; Complex representation, waves of constant frequency; Plane Traveling Waves; Speed of Sound; Acoustic Energy, Intensity and Source Power; Spherical Waves.

2.     Quantitative measures of Sound: Levels and Decibel; Frequency Analysis; Frequency Weighting and Filters; Fourier Series and Transfer Functions.

3.     Monopoles, Dipoles, Multipoles: Monopoles and Green's Functions; Dipoles and Quadrupoles; Multipoles, radiation efficiency, and the influence of source motion; Reciprocity;

4.     Aerodynamic Sound in unbounded flows: Aerodynamic sound; Lighthill's acoustic analogy; The Ffowcs Williams-Hawkings equation.; Curle's theory; Moving sources; Jet noise and its control. 

5.     Sound generation in a fluid with rigid boundaries: Influence of rigid boundaries; Special cases treated by the method of compact Green's functions; Boundary layers and boundary layer noise; Trailing edge noise; Sound generation by moving surfaces - rotor noise.

6.     Special topics: Automobile aeroacoustics; cavity noise; Computational aeroacoustic
