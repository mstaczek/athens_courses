River energy within climate change framework: from hydropower to floods, sediment transport and water temperature

Objectives:

The course combines the basic theory of Open Channel Flow, River Hydraulics and Engineering, with hands-on experience on computer-based applications.

The course makes extensive use of simulation software HEC-RAS, a free software available at the US Army Corps of Engineers (https://www.hec.usace.army.mil/software/hec-ras/), used worldwide to perform calculations of river flow, flood routing, sediment transport and temperature/water quality analysis. Additional computations are also performed outside HEC-RAS, using problems/exercises and/or a spreadsheet software (e.g. MS Excel).

A step-by-step modelling approach is followed to analyse a test-case of inundation following a dam-break scenario, with emphasis on mitigating the damage: weather forecast, reservoir operation, dam surveillance, vulnerability reduction, flood management plans.

Students will have to pass a final three-hour test, designing and performing hydraulic simulations in HEC-RAS for a given study case; computer aided (documentation is allowed).

Programme to be followed:

The five-day course consists of:

Lectures (2 hours daily) with interactive discussion and questions

Computer simulation practice (4 hours, daily) with HEC-RAS; additional computations are performed with MS Excel.

1.     Introduction to River Hydraulics and Energy in the framework of climate change

Flow regimes, 1D and 2D flow equations, Stream Power, influence of climate change on precipitations, river discharge, hydropower, affected urban areas

2.     River flow modeling

Equations, Simplifying assumptions, Numerical methods, Data, Conditions, Results

3.     Types of hydropower plants. Modeling flow over / through dam spillways.

Type of spillways and flow equations. Flood attenuation in reservoirs. Modeling schematization of dam spillways, equations, data, results.

4.     River sediment transport and modeling; River water temperature and modeling in the climate change framework


Course exam:

3 hours at the end of the 5th day, computer-assisted (documentation allowed)
