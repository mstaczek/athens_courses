Course title:
ENGINEERING OPEN-SOURCE MEDICAL DEVICES.
Type of course:
Classic ATHENS course
Course code:

Minimum year of study:
3
Language of tutition:
English
 
Prerequisites:

Interest in medical technology development.

Interest in computer-aided design technologies.

Interest in rapid prototyping (i.e. 3D printing, 4D printing, Arduino) technologies.

No special requisites apart from basic engineering knowledge or, alternatively, notions of biology and medicine.

All engineering specialties are welcome.

Students from other areas, including medicine and biological sciences are also welcome.
Objectives:
The course introduces the “engineering of open-source medical devices” and is organized as a one-week design school with 3 hours of lessons and seminars and 3 hours of practical design work in teams. In one week, teams of students will live through the specification, conceptual design, preliminary prototyping and testing of different medical devices, and through hands-on activities learn the fundamentals of medical technology engineering. New co-creation methods and considerations for commercialization of medical technology, both in the EU and in low- and middle-income settings, will be experienced. Teamwork skills and design and manufacturing abilities will be fostered through a project-based learning methodology.
Programme to be followed:

Lessons:

-          Engineering methodology for successful medical technologies.

-          Open-science and open-innovation in healthcare.

-          Design and manufacturing technologies for healthcare technologies.

-          Biomedical materials for the smart implants of the future.

-          Research trends: tissue engineering and biofabrication.

Practical seminars:

-          Teamwork and project management.

-          Creativity promotion.

-          Introduction to digital flow in healthcare: print your own design.

-          Introduction to Arduino for rapid prototyping involving biosignals.

-          Artificial intelligence for innovative medical devices.

Teamwork sessions:

-          Medical need identification and product specification.

-          Medical devices classification and detection of applicable standards.

-          Conceptual design and computational modelling.

-          Rapid prototyping and testing.

-          Communication of results.

Visits to UPM’s laboratories on: Product Development, Composites and Nanocomposites, Manufacturing.
Course exam:
Evaluation will rely on students’ teamwork activities during the week, which will result in a final presentation and a conceptual medical device design, demonstrated through a proof-of-concept prototype. There is no written exam, it is a project-based learning course.
