Prerequisites:
The course is aimed at students of design-related careers and consists of a theoretical-practical exercise suitable to develop at all levels. No specific prior knowledge is required.
Objectives:

The general objective of the course is to emphasize the reconciliation between creative design processes and the use of natural resources and recycling practices for the development of new materials for the design industry. An industrial piece will be developed using as raw material pulp and sludge from recycled paper, and applying simple manual manufacturing techniques. A system of acoustic model solutions will be theoretically designed and one piece will be physically produced. For incoming students, it presents a simple introductory exercise for the design of an industrial object, while for more advanced students it poses the challenge of applying the knowledge acquired in profiling specific details such as the geometric conjunction of pieces, joints, manufacturing processes, motif combination problems, texture application, tile thickness combination, etc.

Programme to be followed:

MON:

THEORY: 2h

Welcome session and course presentation. Overall introduction to paper pulp and sludge fabrication processes. Practical examples.

LAB: 4h

Pulp fabrication from recycled paper. Molding of base pieces.   

TUE:

THEORY: 2h

Introduction to design examples using paper as raw material. Design processes.

LAB: 4h

Design session. Mentored individual work on the final piece design. Drying of base pieces.   

WED:

 

THEORY: 2h

Introduction to complementary fabrication processes. 3D, laser cut, smoothing, sanding, cutting, surface mecanization, etc.   

LAB: 4h

Design session. Mentored individual work on the final piece design. Drying of base pieces.   

THU:

 

THEORY: 1h

Visit to FabLab for the mecanization of final pieces. Introduction to FabLab equipment.

 

LAB: 5h

Supervised work on the mecanization of final pieces.  

FRI:

 

THEORY: 2h

Introduction to natural dyes and additives for eco-design practices.

LAB: 4h

Application of natural dyes for the final piece design. Closing session.

Course exam:

The course will be evaluated on the basis of the practical exercise. The evaluations shall particularly assess the ability to generate novel ideas starting from a standard basis of the exercise, the technical resolution of the idea developed in the scheduled time, as well as the propositional potential of the design as the basis for future broader and more detailed developments. Students will be able to finish their work within 30 days after the end of the course and send a presentation in digital format.
