Course title:
Digitalization strategies in the transportation sector for the sustainable promotion of international tourism.
Type of course:
Classic ATHENS course

1

Prerequisites:
Basic knowledge about the economy, transportation, and tourism sector.
Objectives:

Over the past years, the entire travel industry of the modern leisure sector has been facing the challenge of sustainability through investments in more sustainable mobility solutions that can deliver environmental, social, and economic benefits beyond greenhouse gas reductions. Hence, the key issue is encouraging investment in affordable, sustainable, and environmentally friendly transport modes (air, rail, road, and naval for maritime and inland waterways).

SYLLABUS

Lecture topics:

- Impact of air transportation and tourism in different sectors of the economy.

- Identify the main variables affecting air transport and tourism.

- Different methodologies for predictive analysis of demand applied to different flows of pax and tourists, modes of transportation, and flows of cargo and mail.

- Review of various examples based on methodology applied in several cases using hub & spoke strategies.


Practical session:

- Case study analysis where team groups must prepare a presentation on any of the following topics:

• Spanish domestic market: The case of passenger transport services between Madrid and Barcelona, the air corridor versus railway.

• Korean domestic market: Scheduled transport services on the air route between Jeju InternationalAirport (IATA: CJU, ICAO: RKPC) and Gimpo International Airport (IATA: GMP, ICAO: RKS).

• US domestic route: Scheduled transport services on the air route between Harry Reid International Airport (IATA: LAS, OACI: KLAS) and Los Angeles International Airport (IATA: LAX, OACI: KLAX).

•  Main flows of tourism in the EU single market according to each mode of transportation.

•  Air corridors between South America and the EU.
Programme to be followed:

From 13 March 2023 to 17 March 2023

  • One opening session (2 h) including a guided tour lasting some 60 minutes to groups of up to 25 people in the representative building of the Chamber of Commerce in Madrid.
  • Introductory session on the transportation sector and tourism industry (6 h)
  • Three lecture sessions (6 h each).
  • One practical workshop on case studies (6 h).
  • (To be confirmed) Up to two guided tours lasting some 120 minutes to groups of up to 50 people in facilities of leading firms in the transportation sector.
Course exam:
  • Short essay (up to 4 pages).
  • Multiple-choice examination (up to 40 questions)
