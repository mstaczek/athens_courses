Course title:
The Basics of Circular Economy in the Construction Sector context: transforming wastes into resources.
Type of course:
Classic ATHENS course
Course code:
UPM127
Minimum year of study:
3
Language of tutition:
English
 
Prerequisites:

No specific prerequisites. Recommended:

  • Interest in sustainability and circular economy.
  • Some general knowledge on the construction sector.
  • Enthusiasm for contributing and learning within interdisciplinary groups.
  • Participative attitude.
  • It is also recommended to have a laptop.

Objectives:

To increase awareness of students of the choices for a circular economy in the construction sector.

To enhance the student’s transversal skills and commitment to actively learn, participate and contribute in an interdisciplinary context.

Programme to be followed:

The programme tackles the conditions under which end-of-life products can be new resources, becoming secondary raw materials instead of waste. Lectures thus address the importance of building design, construction, use and demolition and decontamination, existing recycling technologies and materials’ characterization throughout the last life cycle stage.

Students will develop a course work on circular economy applied to construction products, which ends with an infographic and a presentation summarizing the results.

Course exam:
  • 20% involvement, valuing class participation and the contribution of innovative ideas to the different activities.
  • 80% collaborative work and final exam.

The competencies sought and assessed in this course are mainly related to critical and innovative thinking, skills in sustainability (green skills), interpersonal skills (for example, presentation and communication skills, organizational skills, teamwork, etc.), intrapersonal skills (for example, self-discipline, enthusiasm , perseverance, self-motivation, etc.), global citizenship (e.g., tolerance, openness, respect for diversity, intercultural understanding, etc.), and media and information literacy (e.g., ability to locate and access the information). All of them in line with the six domains of transversal competences defined by UNESCO, and with the particular objectives of the Circular EELISA Community, to which the course teachers belong.
