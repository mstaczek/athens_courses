Objectives:

MAKING AND REMAKING healthy, more liveable and greener cities.

WHY?

Humanity, especially the people living in the ever growing and densifying cities all over the world are facing major problems related to climate change, flooding, droughts, pollution, noise, water quality, heat island effect. In densified cities, people not only need space in their apartments but they also need public space where they sport, wander, and cycle, relax, commute, and interact with others. In general, we could state this is about the liveability of the environment. Through resilient urban planning and greening, cities can become more pleasant and healthier places to live in.

 

The above description is maybe nog that difficult to grasp, accomplishing is a serious and wicket problem. Nevertheless a great one to work on.


The department of Management in the Built Environment and Landscape Architecture & Urban Forestry of the Delft University of Technology organizes an Athens week about this theme.


Making cities healthier and greener is, on the one hand a matter of design and planning and on the other hand a matter of getting things done. Design and Management go hand in hand. Designing is about knowing your context and knowing what to design. Getting things done relate to the social, technical, ecological, financial, and governmental construct of cities. Getting things done is about understanding and working with multiple stakeholders.

Programme to be followed:

The programme of the week:

During the Athens week, we would like to approach this challenge trough collaborative group work. Groups of 5 to 6 students work on an assignment and a real case in a district in the city of Amsterdam. Students from different background like governance, management, urban planning, landscape architecture, water management, ecology or and agriculture are more than welcome and work together in teams. Students that want to know more about Management in the Build Environment or Landscape Architecture for their Master thesis are able to get to know our university and experience our way of education.

 

We will make a challenging five-day program with attractive daily lectures and themes, daily tasks and a final group assignment presented to a jury. Lectures by scholars from the university and professionals

professionals from practice both broaden the knowledge exchange. We will organise an excursion to the district in Amsterdam for a walking and cycling tour, and lectures on site.

 

We strive for about 32 to 42 students to make groups of about six students.

Course exam:

Final presentation for a professional Jury on Friday afternoon 17 March 2023.
