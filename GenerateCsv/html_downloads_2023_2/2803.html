Course title:
Planning the European electricity system: a serious game

Objectives:
Discover electricity system planning (long-term) studies, and associated models and tools
Programme to be followed:

This course will simulate European electricity system planning (2050) in the form of a serious game. Each group of students will be responsible for designing the investment policy of a specific country, following the typical stages of such a long-term study:

1. Demand “projection”: depending on country description (current electricity usages and potential ones), determine a scenario for future demand: how much “electrification”? For which new usages (electric mobility, heat pumps…)? With what impact on households, industry, etc.?

2. Production capacity: depending on cost and environmental targets (CO2 emissions), make a decision about “Generation Expansion Planning”*: new electricity production assets? Share of renewables/nuclear/thermal?

3. Network(s): after an introduction to basics of behavior/congestion aspects of networks at borders, collectively choose a scenario of future investments on a European scale: interconnection capacities between simulated countries?

4. Supply-demand equilibrium simulation: using a provided tool, schedule production to satisfy demand at minimal cost, on a European scale (“Unit Commitment”*, or “dispatch”* problem).

Observe main metrics: “failure”, (mean) production cost, renewable curtailment. Analyze impact of decisions made at previous stages (of items 1.-3.) on these metrics. Even if some lessons of this course will be “theoretical” (presentation of the physical, economical, and mathematical fundamentals of electricity system modelling), it will mostly be made of simulations/practical work. Simulations will be run to observe the interactions of policies of all group together at the European scale.

* These are key optimization problems of the field you will be introduced to.
