Objectives:

This course aims to provide a practical introduction to physical geology focusing on the use of geological cross-sections and the visual identification of the most common geological materials and rocks. The main objectives are:

To practice drawing geological cross-sections in order to learn the main uses of this revealing tool. The geological cross-sections allow to deduce the geological history and the location of the different materials in a given study area. In order to achieve this objective, it is necessary to study the main geological structures and the geological maps, in addition to know how to integrate the main information into a graphic representation.

To identify most geological materials or rocks in the laboratory and in the field and deduce their main properties.

Programme to be followed:

Day 1. Introduction to cartography, geological maps, igneous rocks

 Geographic coordinate systems.

 Scales and topographic reliefs.

 Geological outcrops.

 Structural elements.

 Geological maps, symbols and keys.

-          Practice 1 Cross. Draw topographic profiles and unfolded materials

-          Practice 1 Rocks. Identification, description and properties of main igneous rocks

 

Day 2. Geological cross-sections of horizontal and folded materials

-          Practice 2 Cross. Cross-sections with different folds.

-          Practice 2 Rock. Identification, description and properties of main metamorphic rocks

Geologic Time, Relative Absolute. Practices exercises.

 Field trip introduction.

 

 

Day 3. Field Trip around Madrid region

 Visit to different geological structures: bedding planes, folds, faults and igneous intrusion

 Identification of the main geological materials

 Understanding of Erosion and Soil formation processes.

 

 

Day 4. Geological section on faulted materials

 Fault types.

-          Practice 3 Cross. Cross-section with normal faults.

-          Practice 4 Cross. Cross-section with reverse faults.

-          Practice 3 Rocks. Sedimentary rocks

 

Day 5. Geological histories and cross sections

Review and correction of all practices .

Final Exam.
Course exam:
The final exam consists of (i) drawing a geological cross-section from a geological map, (ii) describing common rocks in the laboratory and (iii) deduced the geological history of a second geological cross-section. Students will have the option to correct their mistakes and submit the results within 72 hours after the exam.
