Prerequisites:

The students must be familiar with SDGs and be interested in mathematics as a tool to improve our society. The ability to work on group is essential. Some knowledge in data science and computer science might be useful, but it is not compulsory. Students might need to use their personal computer to develop the course.

Objectives:

The objective of the course is to introduce the notion and dynamics of a citizenship laboratory to students and develop one to build bridges in the community to improve our societies at an international level by using mathematics and the links to the Sustainable Development Goals (SDG). Students from EELISA universities are specially welcome.

 

A citizenship laboratory consists of three stages: brainstorming for projects, searching for collaborators andhands-on.

 

First day some selected projects proposed in ExperimentaMates citizenship lab in the last twoeditions (h  ttps://blogs.upm.es/experimentamates/ , the we bsiteisonlyinSpanis h) will be described. But the course is open to receive ideas for projects from the students aligned with the goals of thecourse.

 

Another objective is to work in interdisciplinary teams with the guidance of professors and experts in the topic to design possible prototypes at the hands-on stage.


Programme to be followed:

Day 1: Introduction to EELISA communities: SSERIES. What is a laboratory citizenship?

Presentation of the projects related to: citizen science, mathematics for sustainability, fighting misinformation, etc.

 

Day 2: Work in groups distributed among the different projects together with a mentor for each project.

 

Day 3: Rotation among the projects and brainstorming together. Day 4: Work in original groups.

Day 5: Final presentation and future line works.

 

Schedule: Monday to Friday, 9am-3pm.
Course exam:

There is no exam. The evaluation consists of working in groups developing the different projects and the final presentation of the achievements in each project. Every group will have to hand in a final report one week after the conclusion of the ATHENSâ week.
