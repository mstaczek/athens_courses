Prerequisites:
Students from different fields of studies are welcome expecting their interest and engagement to the discussion between architecture, built environment and cities. Digital communication skills (graphic design and presentations), teamwork orientation and active participation is expected. The students are required to bring a laptop and a digital camera/smartphone for the course.
Objectives:

Urban areas, where most of the world's population will live in 2050, are the heritage we inhabit and for which it is urgent to adopt a sustainable circular design. To achieve a healthy and inclusive environment, is urgent to rethink the way we design and govern. This course proposes a reading that reveals the design of well-being and comfort, based on hygienist, ecological and sustainable basic principles.

The main objectives are:

- Stimulate the understanding of the architecture of health and the city of well-being as emerging phenomena that reflect the political-economic, socio-cultural, technological and artistic structure;

- Know and integrate the architecture of health within the framework of global culture;

- To deepen the knowledge and framing of the architecture of health and the city of well-being over time, stimulating the discussion of the great challenges of the future;

- Awakening to the understanding of history as a necessary operative tool for the invention of the future;

- Stimulate critical reading of contemporary values of comfort;

- Equip students with instruments and methodologies that allow questioning and proposing solutions (reflection, research, self-learning, integration and relationship of knowledge acquired in other areas; autonomous and team work; organization, problematization and communication of concepts and knowledge purchased).
Programme to be followed:
A one-week course based programme focused on theoretical modules, site visits, city walks, study of international case studies, and critical thinking of Lisbon’s case studies. The studio work methodology will be based on fieldwork in the city of Lisbon, discussion based on presentations, films/documentaries and text readings. Students from different fields and backgrounds will be arranged in multidisciplinary groups to discuss existent neighborhoods, their healthy features and future scenarios. The course follows a learning-centred problem solving and experimentation approach rather than purely teacher-centered learning mode. It explicitly calls for students’ engagement, participation and interaction.
