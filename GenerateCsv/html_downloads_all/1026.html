Prerequisites:
Since the students will develop their own mobile application, it is important that they feel comfortable with Java, and that they are able to develop desktop applications. The Eclipse development environment will be used, and practicing it in advance will be very beneficial to the students.
Students willing to bring and use their own laptop during the class may do so, provided they have installed the Eclipse development environment and the Android SDK (software development kit) before starting the course.

Objectives:
Mobile applications (for smartphones, tablets, connected televisions) offer unique possibilities not usually found in desktop computers (e.g., geolocation, touchscreen, ubiquitus connectivity). At the same time, those applications must accommodate extra constraints such as a low energy consumption, disconnected mode, limited input capabilities, or various display sizes and device orientations.
This class will focus on the particulars of mobile applications, using Android as a development platform. Throughout the week, students will develop their own mobile application. Mobile devices will be made available to them during the sessions so that they can field test the application.
