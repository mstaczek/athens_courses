Course title:
Introduction to numerical modelling for river hydraulics
Course code:
POLI10
Minimum year of study:
4
Language of tutition:
english
 
Prerequisites:
The course is addressed at Civil and Environmental Engineering students with good mathematical background on fluid dynamics and basic numerical analysis, especially numerical methods for ordinary differential equations. Some knowledge of MATLAB/OCTAVE is required in order to be able to participate actively to the practical sessions.
Objectives:
The course aims at providing an introduction to the mathematical and numerical tools used in river hydraulics. The fundamental governing equations of river flow will be introduced and the key ideas underlying numerical methods for their approximate solution will be presented. Each theoretical topic will be complemented by practical sessions in which implementations of selected numerical methods in MATLAB or standard commercial codes will be used.
Programme to be followed:
1)  The de Saint Venant equations for open channel flow. Derivation from the three dimensional equations of free surface flow. Friction models.2) Introduction to  finite difference and finite volume discretizations. Concepts of convergence and stability. Examples of classical numerical methods for the advection diffusion equation.3) The Riemann problem for the de Saint Venant equation: shock waves and rarefaction waves. Steady state solutions in the viscous and inviscid case. Finite difference and finite volume numerical methods for the de Saint Venant equations.4) A realistic application
Course exam:
Participants will be evaluated on the basis of their results of a final exercise session devoted to a review of the topics covered in the course.
